## Core Philosophy: Singapore Math Approach

### Concrete â†’ Pictorial â†’ Abstract (CPA)

The world's highest-performing maths students (Singapore, Japan, South Korea) learn through CPA:

1. **Concrete** â€” Use physical objects (blocks, counters, rods)
2. **Pictorial** â€” Draw diagrams, bar models, number lines
3. **Abstract** â€” Use numbers and symbols

**Example: Teaching 13 - 7**
- **Concrete:** Start with 13 blocks. Take away 7. Count what's left.
- **Pictorial:** Draw 13 dots. Cross out 7. Count remaining.
- **Abstract:** 13 - 7 = 6

### Bar Model Method (Singapore)

The bar model is the most powerful problem-solving tool in primary maths:

```
Problem: Tom has 45 marbles. He gives 18 to Sam. How many does Tom have left?

[------ 45 marbles total -------]
[-- 18 given away --][--- ? ---]

45 - 18 = 27 marbles
```

```
Problem: A shop sold 35 red apples and 28 green apples. How many in total?

[-- 35 red ---][-- 28 green --]
[----------- ? total ----------]

35 + 28 = 63 apples
```

Teach this method early â€” it works all the way through to algebra.

## Stage-by-Stage Detail

### Stage 1: Number Sense (Age 5, Kindy)

**Key concepts:**
- Counting to 100 (forward and backward from 20)
- Recognise numbers 0-20
- More than / less than
- Basic 2D shapes
- Simple patterns
- Addition within 10 using objects

**Activities:**
| Activity | How Often | Details |
|----------|-----------|---------|

### Stage 2: Operations (Age 6, Year 1)

**Key concepts:**
- Addition and subtraction facts to 20 (memorise)
- Number bonds to 10 (most critical skill)
- Place value: tens and ones
- Skip counting by 2s, 5s, 10s
- Simple word problems
- Half and quarter concepts
