# üéØ Career Pathways ‚Äî Medicine, Mathematics & Technology

> Building toward future careers in medicine, mathematics, or technology. These pathways share common foundations that start in primary school.

---

## Shared Foundation Skills

All three pathways require:
- **Strong analytical thinking** ‚Äî logic, reasoning, problem-solving
- **Mathematical proficiency** ‚Äî from arithmetic through to advanced math
- **Reading comprehension** ‚Äî absorbing and analysing complex information
- **Discipline and persistence** ‚Äî long-term focus and dedication
- **Curiosity** ‚Äî genuine interest in how things work

These are exactly what the selective school pathway develops.

## ü©∫ Medicine Pathway

### The Long Road
```
Primary School     ‚Üí Strong academics, love of science, empathy
Selective High     ‚Üí High performance, biology, chemistry, advanced maths
HSC (Year 12)      ‚Üí High ATAR (99.5+ for direct entry, 95+ for graduate entry)
University         ‚Üí Medicine degree (5-6 years) OR Science/biomedical + graduate medicine
Internship/Residency ‚Üí 2-5+ years specialisation
```

### What to Build Now (Ages 5-12)

| Skill | How to Develop | Why |
|-------|---------------|-----|
| **Science curiosity** | Experiments at home, nature walks, documentaries | Foundation for biology, chemistry |
| **Strong reading** | Read widely, especially non-fiction | Medical school requires massive reading |
| **Mathematics** | Strong foundations ‚Üí advanced maths | Essential for chemistry, physics, statistics |
| **Empathy** | Books about different experiences, volunteering | Medicine is fundamentally about people |
| **Fine motor skills** | Drawing, building, craft, instrument | Surgery, procedures require dexterity |
| **Communication** | Public speaking, debates, explaining things | Doctor-patient communication is critical |

## üéµ Complementary Activities for All Pathways

Research supports these activities for cognitive development:

| Activity | Benefit | Evidence |
|----------|---------|---------|
| **Musical instrument** | Improves mathematical reasoning, discipline, spatial-temporal reasoning | Multiple longitudinal studies |
| **Chess** | Strategic thinking, patience, planning ahead | Linked to improved maths scores |
| **Physical sport** | Brain health, discipline, teamwork, stress management | Physical activity improves cognitive function |
| **Reading fiction** | Empathy, vocabulary, creativity, comprehension | The single best predictor of academic success |
| **Free play** | Creativity, social skills, problem-solving | Essential for brain development |

### Recommended Balance
```
Academic development:  40% of extracurricular time
Physical activity:     30%
Creative/Music:        15%
Free play/social:      15%
```
