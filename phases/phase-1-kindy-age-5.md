## üìñ The Science of Reading ‚Äî How to Teach a 5-Year-Old to Read

The research is clear: **systematic synthetic phonics** is the most effective method.

### Step-by-Step Approach

#### Month 1-2: Letter Sounds (NOT letter names)
**The biggest mistake:** Teaching "A, B, C" before "ah, buh, cuh". Letter sounds unlock reading; letter names come later.

**How to teach:**
1. Start with the most useful letters first: **s, a, t, p, i, n** (these make many words)
2. Teach 2-3 new sounds per week
3. Use multisensory methods:
   - **See it:** Show the letter on a card
   - **Say it:** Make the sound together
   - **Trace it:** Write it in sand, shaving cream, or with finger paint
   - **Find it:** Spot the letter in books, signs, cereal boxes

**Order to teach letter sounds:**
```
Group 1: s, a, t, p
Group 2: i, n, m, d
Group 3: g, o, c, k
Group 4: e, u, r
Group 5: h, b, f, l
Group 6: j, v, w, x, y, z
Group 7: sh, ch, th, ck (digraphs ‚Äî 2 letters, 1 sound)
```

#### Month 2-3: Blending
Once he knows ~8 letter sounds, start blending:
- **s-a-t** ‚Üí sat
- **t-a-p** ‚Üí tap  
- **p-i-n** ‚Üí pin

**Technique:** Point to each letter, say each sound slowly, then "push" them together faster and faster.

#### Month 3-6: Decodable Books
Move to decodable readers (books where every word can be sounded out with known letters):
- **Dandelion Readers** (UK, excellent phonics sequence)
- **Bob Books** (US, very popular)
- **Little Learners Love Literacy** (Australian!)
- **Jolly Phonics readers**

#### Month 6-12: Build Fluency
- Re-read favourite decodable books for fluency
- Introduce sight words (words that can't be easily sounded out)
- Begin levelled readers (PM Readers, Oxford Reading Tree)

### Daily Reading Routine (15-20 min total)

| Activity | Duration | Details |
|----------|----------|---------|
| Phonics practice | 5 min | Letter sounds, blending activities |
| Read-aloud (parent reads) | 10 min | Rich, interesting stories above reading level |
| Attempt reading (child reads) | 5 min | Decodable books at child's level |

## üî¢ Building Number Sense

### Proven Approach: Concrete ‚Üí Pictorial ‚Üí Abstract (CPA)

This is the **Singapore Math** methodology, used by the world's highest-performing maths students.

#### Concrete (use real objects)
- Count blocks, toys, fruit, stairs
- "Give me 7 blocks" / "How many grapes are on your plate?"
- Use fingers for addition

#### Pictorial (use pictures/drawings)
- Draw dots to represent numbers
- Use number lines on paper
- Colour-in counting worksheets

#### Abstract (use numbers)
- Recognise written numerals
- Write numbers
- Simple number sentences: 3 + 2 = 5

### Key Activities

| Activity | How Often | Tools |
|----------|-----------|-------|
| Counting everyday objects | Daily | Anything! |

## üí° Parent Tips for Age 5

### Do's ‚úÖ
- **Read aloud every day** ‚Äî this is the single most impactful thing you can do
- **Make it fun** ‚Äî games, songs, stories, not worksheets every day
- **Talk constantly** ‚Äî describe what you see, ask questions, expand vocabulary
- **Praise effort**, not intelligence ("You worked hard!" not "You're so smart!")
- **Let him struggle** a little ‚Äî productive struggle builds resilience
- **Visit the library** weekly ‚Äî let him choose books
- **Play board games** ‚Äî mathematical thinking, turn-taking, strategy
- **Be patient with reading** ‚Äî going from zero to reading takes 6-12 months
- **Celebrate small wins** ‚Äî reading his first word is a HUGE milestone

### Don'ts ‚ùå
- Don't compare to other children
- Don't push too hard ‚Äî if he's frustrated, take a break
- Don't skip phonics for "whole language" guessing
- Don't over-schedule ‚Äî free play is critical for brain development
- Don't make reading feel like punishment
- Don't use screens as a babysitter ‚Äî educational apps need parent involvement at this age

## üìä Term-by-Term Targets (Kindy Year)

### Term 1 (Feb-Apr)
- Learn first 12 letter sounds (s, a, t, p, i, n, m, d, g, o, c, k)
- Count to 30
- Recognise numbers 0-10
- Complete 24-piece puzzles

### Term 2 (May-Jun)
- Learn remaining letter sounds + begin digraphs (sh, ch, th)
- Blend CVC words (sat, pin, dog)
- Count to 50
- Add within 5 using objects

### Term 3 (Jul-Sep)
- Read simple decodable books (3-5 words per page)
- Learn 20 sight words
- Count to 70
- Understand basic shapes

### Term 4 (Oct-Dec)
- Read decodable books independently (simple sentences)
- Know 40+ sight words
- Count to 100
- Add and subtract within 10
- Write first name and simple CVC words

*Remember: Every child develops at their own pace. These are targets, not deadlines. The goal is to build a strong foundation and a genuine love of learning.*
